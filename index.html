Welcome to the website for sharing my Music 270 Final Project.

I am undoubtedly not a professional web developer, so please don't expect this website to be mindblowing.

However, it will provide you with a platform to listen to the five beats that I made for this project, each in different styles of hip hop/rap.

First up, the style that I tend toward is trap.

While this is a pretty generic name for a subgenre of hip hop, it can be best identified by a variation of samples with a classic 808 drum pattern w/ quantized 16th note hi hats and 808 bass notes.

This particular piece was inspired by the works of Atlanta producers Wheezy and Turbo. Enjoy.

Up next is my attempt at a NY/UK drill style similar to the beats of axl beats on songs by artists like Fivio Foreign and Pop Smoke.

This style can be recognizd by drum patterns with syncopated, layered hi hats, a very bouncy rhythm, and a synth bass with plenty of glide. The sample or harmonic elements are often dark, minor, and orchestral.

The genre that I have been most excited to experiment with is Reggaeton, a genre that came out of South America, particularly Puerto Rico and Colombia with acts like Bad Bunny and J. Balvin.

It is characterised by syncopated rhythms similar to those of dancehall with punchy, wide drums and occasionally Latin American samples.

My inspiration for this piece was a song called Reloj by Rauw Alejandro and Anuel AA.

The last beat that I made for this project is in the style of boom bap, the style of east-coast 90s rap.

This style can be heard behind rappers like biggie smalls, jay z, etc, and include swung, unquantized rhythms with organic drum sounds and eclectic samples.
